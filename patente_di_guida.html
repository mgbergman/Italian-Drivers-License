🧠 The 10 most powerful exam tricks
1
"Non" = almost always FALSO
"Non è obbligatorio / consentito / necessario" — if the statement implies something dangerous is allowed, it's FALSE.
Ex: "Non è obbligatorio usare le cinture" → FALSO
2
"Sempre" and "mai" are red flags
Absolutes (always/never) are often false — traffic law almost always has exceptions.
Ex: "Si può sempre sorpassare" → FALSO
3
Safety = VERO, danger = FALSO
Statements promoting caution (slow down, yield, stop, use lights) are almost always TRUE. Dangerous or careless behaviour = FALSE.
Ex: "In caso di nebbia bisogna rallentare" → VERO
4
"È vietato" = prohibited → VERO
Questions stating something is prohibited are usually TRUE.
Ex: "È vietato parcheggiare sulle strisce" → VERO
5
"Solo" (only) = likely a trap
The rule usually applies to MORE situations than stated.
Ex: "Il casco è obbligatorio solo in autostrada" → FALSO
6
Alcohol: zero tolerance for new drivers
Any question suggesting neopatentati or under-21s can drink any alcohol is FALSO. Their limit is 0.0 g/L.
Ex: "I neopatentati possono avere 0.5 g/L" → FALSO
7
Pedestrians and cyclists always have priority
Giving priority to pedestrians crossing or cyclists going straight = TRUE. Ignoring them = FALSE.
Ex: "Dare precedenza ai pedoni sulle strisce" → VERO
8
Sign questions: "il segnale raffigurato"
Multiple questions share ONE sign. Triangles = warning, red circles = prohibition, blue squares = information.
Red circle = prohibition → VERO that it prohibits
9
Motorway = stricter rules
U-turns, reversing, stopping on carriageway, pedestrians — all forbidden on autostrada.
Ex: "In autostrada è consentita l'inversione" → FALSO
10
Roundabout: those inside have priority
Vehicles already in the roundabout have right of way. Opposite = FALSE.
Ex: "Nelle rotonde si dà precedenza a chi entra" → FALSO
Learn these ~40 words and you can decode almost any exam question.
